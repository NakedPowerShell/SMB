    Complete SMB Cybersecurity Guide for Windows 7 After Microsoft Support Ends on 01/14/2020

    SMBWriter Published on August 27, 2019

    This complete SMB cybersecurity guide for Win 7 will help you decide if you will upgrade to Windows 10 or stay with Windows 7 after Microsoft support ends on 01/14/2020.

    If you decide NOT to upgrade, I will give a list of security items that you need to implement and pay attention to.

    Extended support for Microsoft Windows 7 ends on January 14, 2020

    No extended support means no more security patches, updates, or fixes. EVER

    Unless you purchase EKU support which can be costly.

    According to Kaspersky, 47% of small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) and enterprises still use Windows 7. Fortunately, none of the organizations Kaspersky has visibility into use Windows XP or Windows Vista, for which support ended years ago, but 0.4% still use Windows 8, which hasn’t received updates since January 2016. (Security Week)

    Intro

     Deciding to upgrade to Windows 10 from Windows 7 or not is a hard decision.

    If you do an upgrade, calculate costs, additional training, and a breaking in time. Do most of your computers even have the resources (RAM, CPU speed, disk space) to upgrade?

    If you upgrade to Win 10, you can review our The Complete SMB Cybersecurity Guide for Windows 10 here.

    If you do NOT upgrade, calculate the tangible and intangible costs and risks, and additional security precautions you will have to take.

    I recommend that you layer your cybersecurity protection so that each layer reinforces the next layer.

      

    Body

    The great risks of a new virus or malware to attach Win 7

    Find an AV vendor that will suport Win 7

    Find vendors of your critical applications that will support Win 7

    Do not allow Win 7 machines to access the Internet

    On your network, segreate the Win 7 machines

    Monitor the Win 7 machines very closely

    Add restrictive permssissions
