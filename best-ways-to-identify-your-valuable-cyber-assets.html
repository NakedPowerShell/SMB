    Best Ways to Identify Your Valuable Cyber Assets Today

    SMBWriter Published on August 18, 2019

    Identifying and understanding which business assets (“digital crown jewels”) and physical assets that others want

    The first step in protecting a business from cyber threats is to identify the “crown jewels” of your business – those assets and systems that are critical to your business.

    Crown jewels assets are the data without which your business would have difficulty operating and/or the information that could be a high-value target for cybercriminals.


    [maxbutton name="CTA1"]

    Quick Wins

    • Create a detailed inventory list of data and physical assets and update it routinely. Record the manufacturer, make, model, serial number and support information for hardware and software. For software, know the specific version that is installed, license numbers, manufacturer. 
    • Know where data and technology are stored and who has access to both.

    We will help you collect a complete inventory of all your software and hardware that is critical to your business continuity.

    Then we can help you analyze the risk to each item and determine the remediation action to correct the risk.
